Secure Election Setup

Recommended election flow

Follow this order to avoid blocked actions later.

1. Create election

Set the title, description, venue, and voting window. New elections begin as drafts.

2. Add positions

Define ballot roles such as President, Treasurer, or committee seats.

3. Add candidates

Attach active candidates to each position before voters receive credentials.

4. Upload roster

Import eligible voters, review billing, pay the invoice, and lock the roster.

5. Issue credentials

Generate single-use voting tokens and deliver them to eligible voters.

6. Open, close, tally

Open voting, close when finished, tally results, then publish and certify.

Step 1: Configure balloting and positions

Lay down the democratic framework.

Build the structure of your ballot before registering voters. A solid balloting setup requires:

  • Define executive positions: Create clearly structured categories such as President or Treasurer.
  • Configure max choices: Specify whether voters can choose one or multiple candidates for each role.
  • Nominate candidates: Ensure every position has active candidates associated with it.

Step 2: Upload roster and settle invoice

Lock down your registry and activate the portal.

To protect the integrity of the election, the eligible voter roster must be paid for and locked:

  • CSV roster upload: Import voters with external ID, name, email, and phone fields.
  • Graduated pricing: Review the billing fee based on your total registry size.
  • Pay with Paystack: Pay via mobile money or card directly from the invoice page.

Step 3: Issue cryptographic voter tokens

Zero-trust, secure ballot delivery.

Once the invoice is settled and your roster is locked, you can bulk-issue secure credentials:

  • Unique single-use tokens: The system generates secure voting credentials for each eligible voter.
  • Automated delivery: Voters receive credential codes and private voting links where contact data is available.
  • Voter secrecy: Tokens are hashed before storage so a ballot cannot be traced back to a voter by administrators.

Step 4: Close, tally, and audit receipts

Transparent results with private ballots.

When voting ends, close the channel, tally results, and publish certified reports:

  • Ballot verification: Voters can use private receipt codes to verify that their ballot was counted.
  • Audit registry: Public receipt listings preserve ballot secrecy while proving counted ballots were not fabricated.
  • Certification: Certify final results once the tally and public report have been reviewed.
